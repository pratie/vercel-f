Not all searches are created equal.
"What is email marketing?" -> Educational intent (Top of Funnel).
"Best email marketing tool for Shopify" -> **Buying Intent** (Bottom of Funnel).

To get leads on Reddit, you need to filter for **Buying Intent**.

## The Keyword Framework

We categorize keywords into three buckets:

### 1. Problem-Aware (High Intent)
The user knows they have a problem but doesn't know the solution.
*   "How to fix [Problem]"
*   "[Problem] workaround"
*   "Struggling with [Task]"

**Strategy:** Reply with advice, then mention your tool as the "easy button."

### 2. Solution-Aware (Higher Intent)
The user knows solutions exist but is comparing options.
*   "[Competitor A] alternative"
*   "Best tool for [Task]"
*   "Is [Product] worth it?"

**Strategy:** These are your hottest leads. Be the alternative they are looking for. Compare features honestly.

### 3. Brand-Aware (Highest Intent)
They are asking about *you*.
*   "[Your Brand] pricing"
*   "[Your Brand] vs [Competitor]"
*   "Is [Your Brand] legit?"

**Strategy:** Jump in immediately! Answer questions, offer a demo, or clarify pricing.

## Building Your Keyword List

Start with your competitors.
List every competitor you have.
Add "alternative", "vs", "pricing", "review" to their names.
`[Competitor] alternative`
`[Competitor] review`
`[Competitor] vs [Your Brand]`

These are the keywords that pay the bills. **SneakyGuy** specializes in detecting these high-intent signals.
