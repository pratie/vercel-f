Reddit has a "spam" problem. But it's not the bots; it's the lazy marketers.
"Great post! Check out my site!" -> Spam.
"This is irrelevant. By the way, I sell X." -> Spam.

If you cross the line, your domain gets banned from entire subreddits, or worse, site-wide shadowbanned.

## The "Value First" Framework

Before you hit "Post," ask yourself:
**"If I remove the link/mention of my product, is this comment still useful?"**

*   **If YES:** It's genuine engagement.
*   **If NO:** It's spam.

### Example: The Developer Query

**Post:** "I'm struggling to set up authentication in Next.js."

**Spam Reply:**
> "Use Auth0, it's easy. Here's a link." (Low value, just a referral).

**Genuine Reply:**
> "NextAuth is tricky at first. Are you using the App Router? If so, make sure you wrap your layout in the SessionProvider. Here is a snippet of how I did it:
> `[Code Snippet]`
>
> If you want something managed, Clerk or Auth0 are easier alternatives, but NextAuth gives you full control."

**Notice:** The genuine reply answers the specific technical question *before* suggesting alternatives.

## Reading the Room

Every subreddit has a unique culture.
*   **r/startups:** Skeptical, hates success theater, loves hard numbers.
*   **r/marketing:** Loves case studies, hates "guru" talk.
*   **r/funny:** Just wants to laugh. Zero tolerance for promotion.

**Rule of Thumb:** Lurk for a week before posting. Read the top posts of all time. Understand the inside jokes and the tone.

## Formatting Matters

A wall of text is ignored. A well-formatted post is read.
*   Use **Bold** for emphasis.
*   Use bullet points for lists.
*   Break up paragraphs (2-3 sentences max).

Readable content = Upvotes = Visibility.

## Conclusion

You can sell on Reddit. You can market on Reddit. But you have to earn the right to do it. Provide value, be human, and treat the community with respect, and they will reward you.
